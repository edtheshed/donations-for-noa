If you are not able to give blood, or would like to do more, you can also support a good cause. The money raised will go
to the Malaria Consortium. You can donate via our [Justgiving page](https://www.justgiving.com/page/donations-for-noa).

Malaria kills around 600,000 people annually, mostly children under 5 in sub-Saharan Africa and Asia. While there isn't
a very strong link between our story and Malaria Consortium, I remain someone who believes in the importance of good
evidence when making donation decisions - and the evidence for the effectiveness of malaria prevention and treatment is
overwhelming. Seasonal malaria chemoprevention, where children are provided with a full course of antimalarial medicines
prophylactically during peak malaria season, can protect a child for as little as £5.

So please trust me that this is a good use of your money. Losing a child is always a tragedy, wherever it occurs, but
particularly when it could have easily been prevented if low-cost medicines were more widely available.
